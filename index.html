You are not broken. You are becoming.

If you've ever been called too loud, too sensitive, too ambitious, or too much—this book is your invitation to rise.

Unapologetically Too Much is the story of a woman who had it all on paper—marriage, career, success—but was dying inside. Through visceral storytelling and transformative rituals, author Zaria Frances Rose chronicles her journey from survival to sovereignty, from burnout to embodied power.

This isn't just a memoir. It's a movement. A mirror. A call to arms for every woman ready to stop apologizing and start becoming.

What Readers Will Discover

Break Free from Blueprints

How to identify and destroy inherited "blueprints" that keep women small and create your own rules for living.

The Sacred Power of No

Learn to say no without explanation and yes without guilt—the foundation of authentic boundaries.

Body-Based Healing

Practical rituals for returning to your body and reclaiming your voice through embodied practices.

Survival vs. Living

Understand the crucial difference between merely surviving and truly living—and how to make the shift.

Your "Too Much" is Enough

Discover why everything they called "too much" about you is actually your greatest source of power.

Transformative Rituals

Step-by-step practices for integration, healing, and embodying your authentic power.
